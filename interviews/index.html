Technical Conversations Dialogues on Craft

A decade of dialogues with the engineers, authors, and community builders who shaped the modern software landscape.

This collection preserves nearly 200 conversations recorded at the inflection points of the Ruby and Software Craftsmanship movements. These aren't just technical interviews; they are snapshots of how we thought about architecture, community, and the ethics of building software during a transformative era.

The Archive:

  • Voices of Influence: Deep-dive dialogues with industry pioneers like Rich Hickey, Robert 'Uncle Bob' Martin, Jez Humble, Aaron Bedra, and many more.
  • Core Themes: The rise of Agile and Software Craftsmanship, the evolution of distributed systems, and the discipline of building resilient, maintainable software.
  • Searchable Forensic History: Every audited interview features a structured, chat-style transcript, extracted technical insights, and YouTube-optimized metadata.
