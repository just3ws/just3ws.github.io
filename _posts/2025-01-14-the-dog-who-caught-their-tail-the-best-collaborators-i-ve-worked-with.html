Some people step into new roles like a dog catching its tail—they’ve achieved something they’ve worked hard for, but the real test comes after the title. Among the best collaborators I’ve worked with are those who make this transition not just about themselves, but about the people around them. They don’t just take on responsibility; they elevate everyone involved.

This isn’t about avoiding mistakes—it’s about embracing growth and collaboration. These are the traits that make someone truly great to work with.

The Strengths of Outstanding Leaders

The best people in new roles have a way of turning potential vulnerabilities into opportunities for connection. They do this by focusing on a few key behaviors:

  • Sharing Context: They understand that information is most valuable when it’s shared. Instead of hoarding knowledge, they ensure everyone is aligned and equipped to contribute meaningfully.
  • Seeking Input: They’re not afraid to admit they don’t know everything. By inviting perspectives, they build trust and create space for better solutions.
  • Celebrating Collaboration: They recognize that working with others isn’t a weakness—it’s the key to collective success.

These behaviors build trust, foster innovation, and make collaboration not just productive but genuinely enjoyable.

The Impact of Great Collaborators

When someone approaches leadership with humility and openness, it transforms the way teams function. Here’s what I’ve noticed about the best collaborators:

  • They Remove Obstacles: By sharing context and being transparent, they make it easier for everyone to do their best work.
  • They Build Trust: Open communication creates an environment where people feel safe to share ideas, admit mistakes, and ask for help.
  • They Inspire Confidence: By valuing input and celebrating contributions, they make their teammates feel seen and appreciated.

This dynamic doesn’t just improve outcomes—it strengthens relationships and creates a sense of shared purpose.

How to Foster Collaborative Intersections

The magic happens at the intersections where collaboration thrives. Here’s what the best people I’ve worked with do to make that happen:

For New Leaders

  1. Share Context Freely
  2. Ask for Input
  3. Celebrate Wins Together

For Teammates

  1. Be Proactive in Offering Support
  2. Focus on Solutions
  3. Build Relationships Through Transparency

What Great Collaboration Looks Like

The best collaborators understand that leadership isn’t about having all the answers. It’s about creating an environment where everyone can thrive. They model humility, transparency, and curiosity, which in turn inspires their teammates to do the same.

Collaboration is a two-way street. When leaders and teammates bring their best selves to the table, the result is more than just successful projects—it’s a culture of trust and mutual growth.

Closing Thoughts

If you’re stepping into a new role, remember that your title isn’t the end of the journey—it’s the beginning. And if you’re working with someone in that position, focus on creating collaborative intersections where both sides can learn and grow.

The best people I’ve worked with know this: Together, we’re not just chasing tails—we’re building something better.
