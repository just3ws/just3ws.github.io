# Voice Lexicon

Use this file to keep tone consistent across homepage, archive, community, and index page rewrites.

## Preferred Vocabulary
- archive
- lineage
- body of work
- craft
- practice
- stewardship
- working group
- ecosystem
- practitioner
- durable work
- tradeoff
- signal
- context
- continuity

## Use With Care
- legacy: use for neutral technical continuity, not nostalgia bait
- veteran: use sparingly and only when context supports it
- authority: show through precision, not status language

## Avoid Terms and Patterns
- world-class
- cutting-edge
- revolutionary
- game-changing
- amazing
- incredible
- thought leader
- rockstar
- ninja
- disrupt
- hustle
- personal brand
- must-watch
- viral

## Substitution Patterns
- "amazing speakers" -> "experienced practitioners"
- "great event" -> "working session for practitioners"
- "industry leaders" -> "engineers and organizers with sustained practice"
- "passionate community" -> "community of regular contributors"
- "insightful content" -> "practical discussion grounded in experience"

## Framing Rules
- Prioritize what was built, discussed, or learned.
- Tie significance to craft, mentorship, or ecosystem continuity.
- Avoid celebrity framing.
- Avoid nostalgia as the primary frame.
- Keep one eye on systems and long-arc consequences.

## Anti-Pattern Checks
- Breathless cadence with stacked adjectives.
- Empty claims not anchored to page artifacts.
- Overuse of rhetorical questions.
- Sales language on non-commercial archive pages.
- Exclamation points.
- Em dash punctuation.

## Before and After Samples

Weak:
"A collection of amazing interviews with world-class speakers."

Revised:
"This archive documents interviews with experienced practitioners across Ruby and software craftsmanship communities, with emphasis on decisions, tradeoffs, and practice."

Weak:
"SCMC was an incredible meetup full of passionate developers."

Revised:
"Software Craftsmanship North America met as a working group of practitioners focused on deliberate improvement, practical talks, and sustained peer learning."

Weak metadata:
"Awesome interviews from conferences and meetups."

Revised metadata:
"Conference and community interview archives documenting how experienced engineers approach craft, mentorship, and long-term software practice."
