# Content Strategy: Technical Engineering Archive

## 1. Business Context & Objective
- **Product:** A curated technical forensic archive of 200+ technical interviews (2006-2026).
- **Primary Goal:** Establish technical authority and leadership in the Ruby/JVM and Software Craftsmanship domains.
- **Target Audience:** Staff Engineers, Engineering Leaders, and Tech Historians.

## 2. Content Pillars
1.  **Architectural Archeology:** Forensic analysis of past technical decisions and their modern relevance.
2.  **The Craftsmanship Origin Story:** The history of SCNA, GOTO, and Chicago Alt.NET.
3.  **JVM & Ruby Renaissance:** Tradeoffs from the era of JRuby, Clojure, and the Rails explosion.
4.  **Technical Mentorship:** Intimate stories on building communities and developing talent.

## 3. Execution Plan
### Phase 1: Foundational "Meta" Content
- **Meta-Post:** "214 technical conversations: What I learned from the creators of the modern stack."
- **Narrative:** Why the archive exists and the "Forensic" approach to technical memory.

### Phase 2: Topical Clusters (Hub & Spoke)
- **Hub:** "The Chicago Software Craftsmanship Movement (2010-2015)."
- **Spokes:** Featured interviews with Sergio Pereira, Robert Martin, and Dave Hoover.

### Phase 3: Creator Series (SEO Optimized)
- High-visibility landing pages for Rich Hickey, Jez Humble, and DHH interviews.

## 4. Distribution Strategy
- **LinkedIn/X Threads:** Using "The Lost Tapes" hook for individual high-value interviews.
- **Engineering Insights:** Injecting curated insights into current Mike Hall technical writing.
